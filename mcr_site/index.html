The Life and Lore of My Chemical Romance

Founding and Members

My Chemical Romance is an American rock band from New Jersey. The band's current lineup consists of lead vocalist Gerard Way, lead guitarist Ray Toro, rhythm guitarist Frank Iero, and bassist Mikey Way. The band was formed by vocalist Gerard Way and drummer Matt Pelissier in New Jersey, soon after the September 11 attacks. Witnessing the World Trade Center towers fall influenced Way's life to the extent that he decided to start a band. Matt Pelissier drummed for the band on their first two albums before being replaced with Bob Bryar. Bryar was the drummer for the band until leaving in 2010 for personal reasons. Jarrod Alexander has been drumming for the band since 2011, through their album "The Foundations of Decay", and during their recent tour.

Albums and Releases

My Chemical Romance has released four studio albums, as well as multiple EPs. Their first studio album, "I Brought You My Bullets, You Brought Me Your Love", was released in 2002. Their second studio album, "Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge", was released in 2004. Their third studio album, "The Black Parade", was released in 2006. Their fourth studio album, "Danger Days: The True Lives of the Fabulous Killjoys", was released in 2011. They've also released several EP's, including "Conventional Weapons" in 2013 and "The Foundations of Decay in 2022."
